Tree Pose

Vrikshasana (वृक्षासन)

About This Yoga

Vrikshasana, or Tree Pose, is a standing balance pose that mimics the steady stance of a tree. This ancient yoga helps develop physical and mental steadiness, teaching us to root down while reaching up.

Health Benefits

  • Improves balance and stability
  • Strengthens thighs, calves, ankles, and spine
  • Stretches the groins, inner thighs, chest, and shoulders
  • Relieves sciatica and reduces flat feet
  • Increases focus and mental clarity
  • Calms the central nervous system

Helps With

Poor Balance Weak Legs Flat Feet Sciatica Stress & Anxiety Lack of Focus

Best Time to Practice

Morning (Recommended)

Practice during early morning (5-7 AM) on an empty stomach for best results. Can also be practiced in the evening, 4-6 hours after a meal.

Precautions

Avoid with recent knee or hip injuries, severe arthritis, or high blood pressure. Those with vertigo should practice near a wall.

Warrior Pose

Virabhadrasana (वीरभद्रासन)

About This Yoga

Virabhadrasana, named after the fierce warrior Virabhadra, is a powerful standing pose that builds strength, stamina, and confidence. It embodies the spirit of a warrior — grounded yet dynamic.

Health Benefits

  • Strengthens shoulders, arms, legs, and back
  • Opens hips, chest, and lungs
  • Improves focus, balance, and stability
  • Stimulates digestion
  • Increases stamina and endurance
  • Therapeutic for carpal tunnel syndrome

Helps With

Weak Stamina Poor Digestion Tight Hips Back Pain Low Energy

Best Time to Practice

Morning or Evening

Best in the morning to energize or evening to release tension. Ensure stomach is empty or practice 4-6 hours after a meal.

Precautions

Avoid with high blood pressure, heart problems, or knee/hip injuries. Those with neck issues should keep gaze forward.

Mountain Pose

Tadasana (ताडासन)

About This Yoga

Tadasana is the foundation of all standing poses. Like a mountain, this pose teaches steady strength and grounded presence. Mastering it involves deep awareness of alignment, breath, and subtle muscle engagement.

Health Benefits

  • Improves posture and body alignment
  • Strengthens thighs, knees, and ankles
  • Firms abdomen and buttocks
  • Relieves sciatica and reduces flat feet
  • Increases awareness and concentration
  • Creates space in the spine, relieving tension

Helps With

Poor Posture Flat Feet Back Pain Fatigue Breathing Issues

Best Time to Practice

Anytime

Tadasana can be practiced at any time of day. Excellent as a starting pose, between other poses as a reset, or throughout daily activities.

Precautions

Those with headaches, insomnia, or low blood pressure should be cautious. People with joint hypermobility should avoid locking knees.

Namaste / Prayer Pose

Pranamasana (प्रणामासन)

About This Yoga

Pranamasana, or Prayer Pose, is a gesture of reverence and gratitude. Palms together at the heart center symbolize balance of opposing forces and unity of mind, body, and spirit. Traditionally used to begin and end yoga practice.

Health Benefits

  • Calms the mind and reduces stress
  • Improves focus and concentration
  • Promotes inner harmony and balance
  • Gently stretches wrists and finger joints
  • Prepares body and mind for meditation
  • Cultivates gratitude and mindfulness

Helps With

Stress & Anxiety Wrist Stiffness Poor Focus Sleep Issues High Blood Pressure

Best Time to Practice

Morning & Evening (Ideal)

Practice during sunrise (4-6 AM) for spiritual benefits, or at sunset. Can also be used anytime you need to center yourself.

Precautions

Very gentle pose with minimal contraindications. Those with severe wrist injuries may keep hands slightly apart.
